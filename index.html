🧳 What to Wear & Packing Guide

Early April in Orlando: mornings in the mid-60s°F, afternoons hot and muggy (80–86°F), evenings cool to low 70s. Disney's indoor A/C is aggressive — the jump from 83°F outside to freezing indoors is jarring. Layer accordingly.

👟 Clothing & Footwear

Moisture-wicking t-shirts (4–5 per person) — avoid plain cotton for all-day park wear
Comfortable walking shorts
Light zip-up hoodie or thin cardigan — for evenings and fierce indoor A/C
Broken-in walking shoes — most important item. DO NOT wear new shoes. 15,000–20,000+ steps/day.
Water shoes or old trainers for Tiana's Bayou Adv (Apr 2) & Kali River Rapids (Apr 3)
Swimwear for Contemporary & Four Seasons pools
1–2 nicer casual outfits for Four Seasons Ravello dinners

☀️ Sun & Heat Protection

SPF 50+ sunscreen — reapply every 2 hours. UV index in April: HIGH (7–8)
Wide-brim hats or caps for all 4 family members
Sunglasses for everyone
Battery-powered neck fans or personal cooling fans — game-changer in 83°F heat in queues
Reusable water bottles — Disney provides free ice water at quick service locations

🎒 Day Bag Essentials

Lightweight day pack (one bag for the family)
Cheap rain ponchos for all 4 (~$2–3 each from Amazon vs $12+ in parks)
Halal-certified snacks: protein bars, nuts, dried fruit, crackers
Blister bandages (essential!), pain reliever, antacids, prescriptions
Portable phone charger / power bank — you will drain your battery every park day
Hand sanitizer and wipes
Dry bags or zip-locks for phones on splash rides
